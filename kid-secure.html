InsuraZen Kid Secure Plan


What is the InsuraZen Protection Insurance Kid Secure Plan?

At Insurazen, we understand that your child's dreams and aspirations are at the center of your heart. Our Kid Secure Plan, a flagship offering under our comprehensive protection insurance solutions, is designed with one goal in mind – to safeguard your child's future and provide you with peace of mind.

The Kid Secure Plan is more than just insurance; it's a promise to protect your child's financial well-being and educational aspirations. This plan is meticulously crafted to provide financial security and support so that your child can reach for the stars.

Features

Financial Protection for your child in the event of unforeseen circumstances

Customizable Coverage, whether it's college tuition, a dream career, or any other life milestone

Tax-Efficient Benefits to enhance your child's financial security.

Flexible Premiums to accommodate your evolving financial circumstances

Guaranteed Insurability allowing you to secure your child's future while they are still young

Eligibility Criteria


For Policyholders (Parents or Legal Guardians):

Age Requirements: Parents or legal guardians applying for the Kid Secure Plan must typically fall within a certain age range, 18 to 65 years old

Residency: Policyholders must be residents or citizens of the region where the plan is offered. Proof of residency or citizenship may be required.

Legal Capacity: Parents or legal guardians must have the legal capacity to enter into a contractual agreement on behalf of the insured child. They are responsible for paying the premiums and managing the policy.

Relationship to the Insured Child: Policyholders should be the legal parents or guardians of the insured child. They have the primary responsibility for the child's financial well-being.



For the Insured Child:

Age Requirements: Depending on the specific terms of the Kid Secure Plan, the insured child need to fall within a certain age range, infancy to young adulthood.

Health Assessment: Depending on the chosen coverage and policy options, a health assessment for the insured child may be required.

Legal Capacity: The insured child should have the legal capacity to be covered by an insurance policy. Minors are typically eligible for coverage under the policyholder's guidance.

Documentation: Policyholders may be required to provide necessary documentation, such as the child's birth certificate or proof of legal guardianship, to facilitate the policy application.

Designated Beneficiaries: Policyholders should designate the child as the primary beneficiary of the Kid Secure Plan, ensuring that the insurance proceeds are directed to the child's financial security and well-being.
